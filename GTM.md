# Go-To-Market Strategy

## Exact target user

Not "startups." The specific person: **an engineering manager or CTO at a 10–50 person Series A startup** who owns the tools budget but doesn't have a dedicated finance person auditing SaaS spend. They approved the AI tool subscriptions 6-18 months ago when the team was smaller, never revisited them, and now have 3-4 overlapping subscriptions that made sense when 2 people were experimenting but are now just line items nobody questions.

Secondary: **a solo founder or early-stage (seed) founder** who is personally paying for AI tools on a personal credit card and has vague guilt that they might be overspending but no baseline to compare against.

**What they don't do:** they don't Google "how to reduce AI spend." They're not thinking about this problem explicitly. The tool needs to reach them in context, not through search intent.

---

## What they Google or scroll through before wanting this

- "cursor vs github copilot 2025" — actively comparing tools before renewal
- "claude pro worth it team" — questioning a specific subscription
- HN: "Show HN: I audited our AI tool spend" — pattern of sharing cost breakdowns gets lots of engagement on HN
- Twitter/X: "how much does your team spend on AI per developer"
- Y Combinator forums on "SaaS spend" or "burn rate"
- r/ExperiencedDevs or r/cscareerquestions threads on "what AI tools does your team use"

## First 100 users in 30 days, $0 budget

**Week 1: Reddit seeding (20-40 users)**
Post a "what I found when I audited our team's AI spend" thread on r/ExperiencedDevs. Not an ad — a real writeup of the findings (e.g., "we were paying for Cursor Business for 3 people and Pro would have been identical"). Link to SpendLens naturally in the comments as "I built this to do it automatically." Reddit hates ads but loves useful tools shared authentically. Expected: 2-5k impressions, 20-40 signups if the post resonates.

**Week 1: HN Show HN (20-50 users)**
"Show HN: SpendLens — free AI tool spend audit for engineering teams." The HN audience is exactly this user. A good Show HN with genuine product utility gets 200-500 upvotes and 5-15% conversion to try the tool. Goal: 20-50 audits.

**Week 2: Twitter thread (15-30 users)**
Write a thread: "We audited 50 startup AI stacks. Here's what we found." Use data from the first week's audits (anonymized). This type of data-driven thread gets shared by CTOs and engineering managers. End with the tool link. Target: 30-60k impressions at low cost.

**Week 2-3: Direct outreach to YC companies (10-20 users)**
Credex has an existing relationship with the YC ecosystem. Direct cold DM to 30 YC W24/S24 founders: "Hey, we built a free AI spend audit — 2 minutes, no login. Curious what you'd find on your stack." This is warm, not spam. Expected conversion: ~30-50% try it.

**Week 3-4: Embed in relevant content (10-15 users)**
Pitch to 3-5 indie newsletters (TLDR, Pragmatic Engineer newsletter, Bytes.dev) to mention the tool in "tools we found useful" sections. Cost: $0 if genuinely useful. These placements reliably drive 200-500 clicks.

## The unfair distribution channel

Credex's existing relationships with AI infrastructure companies (Anthropic, OpenAI partners) means they can get SpendLens mentioned in vendor-side communications — for example, Anthropic's "how to manage your API costs" documentation or resources. A mention from Anthropic's own resources to their API customers would be unusually high-intent traffic that a typical tool cannot access. This is the channel nobody else can replicate.
